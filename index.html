 RÉSUMÉ

Hi, my name is Chirag Bharadwaj, pronounced SHUH-rahg bah-ruhd-WAHJ (traditional: Chirāg Bhāradvāj).

I do a lot of things in my life, but right now the biggest one is probably serving my time as a computer science undergraduate at Cornell University, where I'll be graduating from in May 2017. It can be beautiful, but it can definitely be freezing cold, too. It hit 30 below zero for a few days during both of the past two winters! I take classes in a variety of fields, including mathematics, the sciences, engineering applications, and, of course, computer science. I'm particularly interested in the development and implementation of programming languages.

I'm passionate about learning new things, whether they're about computer science or something completely different, like a well-written composition on the state of the American education system. I don't fit very well into a single category of interests unlike a lot of computer scientists, and I hope to tie some of my interests in engineering, research, and teaching some day to make the next generation of learning much more exciting. More information about me can be found below, but let's talk!

Things that I do at Cornell


Study, Study, Study

I study computer science at Cornell, and am graduating one year early with a B.Sc. I'd be lying if I said that I did not spend a lot of time on the coursework. I have a love/hate relationship with the CS courses at Cornell—they let me learn lots of new things, but they are very rigorous.

Teaching Assistant

I'm a teaching assistant for CS 3410: Computer Systems. I fell in love with the material and the way it was presented when I took the class. I spend an hour or two each day answering students' questions on Piazza, as it's exciting for me to talk about and teach the material.

More teaching!

I'm also a piano teacher for the Cornell Piano Society. I teach two students the foundations of piano, from scales to chords to structured playing and practice. In Fall 2015, I was able to help students learn four major and minor scales as well as play Bach's Minuet in G. I love classical.

Science and Technology

I was a big fan of Science Olympiad back in high school. I made a lot of friends and had friendly encounters during competitions. At university, I help organize invitational events for other high school students so that we can help them experience this, too. I write tests and coordinate events.

Things that I do for fun


Playing with Technology

I like to play around with the next big thing and learn about how we can push boundaries and do amazing things with them. These days, I'm tinkering with Arduino and hardware kits to learn about things at the hardware-software interface. I hope to make some music-related Arduino project sometime soon. Stay tuned!

The Art of Reading

I have been a voracious reader since a child. I probably owe my terrible vision to too many books during late nights in the darkness. One of my resolutions for 2016 is to read a book every week. I don't have the time to choose them, so I decided to let Reddit choose for me instead. I think it will be fun and challenging to do it.

Autodidactism

One of the biggest things I learned in high school was how to teach myself and be honest about what I know and don't know. I've applied that to how I approach learning today, which makes learning new things fun (while keeping the challenge alive). I am now learning Japanese, among other interesting projects. I live to learn.

Performing and Composing

When I was in junior high school, one of my dreams was to attend Julliard and study music. My music instructor let me know that making a livelihood with such a profession was difficult, and he had to work two jobs at night to keep up. I gave up going pro, but I still perform and compose music as time permits, just as I did back then.
