From humble beginnings...

I was born in Flushing, a diverse (and increasingly-gentrified) neighborhood in the borough of Queens in New York City. I grew up there for a little while, and the culture and livelihood of the area has certainly shaped much of who I am today. I don't think I'll ever forget the parks.

My story

Since the Flushing days, I've rocketed on towards bigger things as my life has progressed. You can find a snapshot of some of the highlights of my life in the timeline below. I view these as key events that really changed part of views or my character in some way over the years.

1996
Born
1996
Born in Flushing, NY. I was born on 23 November 1996 at the Flushing Hospital Medical Center, a tiny hospital on the corner of two bustling streets. It was an amazing place to live in. I miss it dearly.
2004
Started learning Spanish
2004
What started out as a requirement in school quickly became an obsession for me. I was exposed to many natural languages as a child, and my parents were somewhat of polyglots themselves. I loved learning another language in school; nay, I just loved school.
2005
Started playing piano
2005
I started to learn the piano around April 2005. I had listened to a lot of Evgeny Kissin and Arthur Rubenstein growing up because of my mother, so I grew interested in playing the piano like these masters had done so before me. It didn't help that I grew up in a very Asian-American area, so many of my peers had been playing awhile already. I fit in better after starting.
2007
Moved to Princeton, NJ
2007
My family moved to Kendall Park, NJ, a town that borders Princeton, NJ. It is an excellent, safe place to grow up and has a high Asian-American population (read: a solid education system). There are natural, preserved areas, and trees line up the entire (forestal) area.
2011
Became a teacher
2011
I realized my passion for teaching around the time I was a sophomore in high school. By this time, I had envisioned and created an independent study program for AP Calculus at my high school, which over twenty students joined over my four years. I taught the entire AP coursework material over the duration of the year. I really realized my potential and my love for it all.
2013
¡Competente en Español!
2013
I became pretty close to professionally-fluent in Spanish after spending nearly ten years on various facets of it in school. I'm proud to say that I also got a 5 on the AP exam, which required solid reading, writing, listening, and speaking abilities. Painstaking...
2014
Graduated high school
2014
I graduated in the top 1% of my class of 750 in high school. I took about 14 AP exams and received 5's on 11 of them and 4's on the other three. I also had an SAT score of 2330, though I guess that scale is outdated now. I was a good student back in the day.
Nowadays, not so much... :)
2015
Started learning Japanese
2015
After learning Spanish so well, I grew interested in teaching myself 日本語 as my fourth language. I was really interested in it especially because it was a serious challenge (as opposed to Spanish, which had phonetic/structural similarities to English). I'm still working on it today, and it's probably some of the most fun I've ever had learning.
2016
Today
Today
Today, I am a senior at Cornell University, studying computer science. (I also occasionally dabble in economics, chemistry, and electrical engineering.) However, I do a lot of other things in my free time (read about them below!).
